Why Kelvin?

Kelvin is the finest temperature scale known to humanity, and common scales such as Celsius and Fahrenheit are lacking much greatness. When temperature is a measure of the average kinetic energy of the particles in an object or medium, you must start your scale AT ZERO so the temperature scales linearly with the energy of the particles. If we were to use Fahrenheit or Celsius, we would be robbing you of important comparative data. Just remember, water freezes at 273.15K, and water boils 100K above that! Also, the surface of the sun is ~5778K!
